The entire economy and societal infrastructure have been built on one premise: What information you know and how you apply it is what makes you valuable.

This made perfect sense in 1950. Imagine the doctor. Nobody could look up their symptoms on the internet. They couldn’t ask Claude or ChatGPT what was going on. So the doctor was so valuable because they had information that nobody else had. Same with the lawyer. The engineer. The strategy consultant. The software developer. And every other professional domain.

The entire economic structure and rules of society were built around it. 

-The education system is built to shove information into your brain & help you process and apply it
-The college and universities are built to credential & verify that you have domain knowledge
-The professional development industry is built on the premise that more information is what you need, and the next insight or piece of content is what will unlock it for you
-The corporate hierarchy built to assemble knowledge workers that aggregated IP and knowledge that was once a tangible competitive advantage

Now, information is abundant. This entire premise breaks and is actively collapsing.

It’s not that what you know isn’t valuable anymore. It’s that it’s no longer scarce. And therefore the premium on what you know is declining based on natural supply & demand dynamics.

The data on this can be assembled:

-75% of the global workforce experiencing consistent burnout and stress
-Entry-level job market is the worst it’s been in 4 decades
-Layoffs are the highest in 1Q 2026 since the bottom of the Great Financial Crisis in 2009
-The value of a college degree is declining faster than the debt used to acquire it

This is the 3rd fundamental economic shift in modern history.

Each time the patterns are very consistent.

And every time, it’s an invitation for humans to elevate to their next level of capacity. It expands economic opportunity for everyone, and there is more economic prosperity than ever before.

This is the decline of the Information Era. Where knowledge and information processing were the scarce premium resource of the economy. Expressed in your conscious mind. Now AI is automating it at a rapid scale.

And the rise of the Frequency Era. Where your frequency is the scarce premium resource. Developed in your subconscious mind and expressed through your nervous system. Leading to the most powerful human capacities that are impossible to replicate by AI: clarity, emotional sovereignty, equanimity, self-trust, discernment, intuition, creativity, deep human connection, vision, autonomy, adaptability, and intentionality.

My new book “The Frequency Era” is out now and invites you to consider a different view of what’s happening in AI, Leadership, Corporate Culture, and Life Satisfaction.

It’s the greatest opportunity of our lifetime.

Success and achievements don't make imposter syndrome go away.

The Fortune 500 executive getting imposter syndrome while preparing for a board meeting. They're Successful. Fully qualified. And still feel like they aren't ready for this level. 

The real estate mogul closing the biggest deal of their career with a voice in their head saying they don't belong in the room with these other people who are too smart and too powerful.

The eight-figure entrepreneur who thought making $10MM would finally make them feel successful, but still doubts themselves, still second-guesses, and still calls their coach before making a major decision.

The speaker who still gets anxiety before a keynote even after doing it 50 times.

Science explains this clearly.

Imposter syndrome, self-doubt, and negative self-talk are not confidence problems. They don't get solved by willpower. 

These are *symptoms* of an unclear or unstable subconscious identity. The internal reference model that determines who you believe yourself to be.

Identity operates automatically. It filters every moment, determines what you reach for. What feels safe versus threatening. What feels easy versus difficult.  And decides what feels like natural expression versus sustained struggle. All in milliseconds before you consciously think or do anything.

Here's a simple way to test yours. I did this with a friend yesterday.

Set a timer for 30 seconds. Write down who you are.

His answer: "ummm......I'm a dad. I'm a salesperson."

Three questions worth asking about your answer:

How long did it take to figure out what to write? 
How intentional were you with the words? 
Does this description match exactly who you want to be?

Having done this with 100+ people, the answer to these questions is almost always no.

Because they've never trained their frequency. 

When identity is clear, stable, and intentionally encoded at the subconscious level, everything changes automatically.

The imposter syndrome stops completely.
The inner critic becomes an inner champion. 
Decisions get faster and cleaner. 
You stop outsourcing your decisions to other people. 
You start showing up in elevated ways without trying.

Identity is not built through affirmations or confidence exercises or willpowering your way through it. It is encoded through structured subconscious training.

Your next breakthrough in performance and life isn’t in a book, on a podcast, or from a coach. It’s trained in your subconscious mind.
